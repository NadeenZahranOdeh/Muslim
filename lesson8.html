What is Obligatory Acts (Wajibat) of Prayer?

The Definition

Wajibat (Obligatory Acts) are actions or duties commanded by Allah that must be fulfilled. In Islamic jurisprudence (Fiqh), their completion earns spiritual reward, while intentionally neglecting them is considered a sin. Depending on the specific act of worship, missing an obligatory duty may sometimes be compensated for, though for others it invalidates the act entirely.

Wajibat of Daily Prayer

Wajibat of Daily Prayer (Salah)In prayer, Wajibat represent specific actions or utterances that sit between voluntary Sunnahs and absolute pillars (Arkān). If a person misses a pillar, the prayer is void. If they accidentally omit a wajib component, the prayer can often be saved by performing the Sujud as-Sahw (prostration of forgetfulness). Common obligatory acts include:Niyyah: The intention prior to starting.Qira'at: Reciting Surah Al-Fatihah and another Surah.Takbirat-ul-Ihram: Saying "Allahu Akbar" to begin.Ruku' and Sujud: Bowing and prostrating.Dhikr: Reciting the specific praises during bowing and prostrating.Tashahhud: The testimony of faith.Salam: The concluding salutation.
